# Guide to Obtaining Best Buy Financial Data

## Primary Data Sources

### 1. SEC EDGAR Database
**URL**: https://www.sec.gov/edgar/searchedgar/companysearch.html
- Search for "Best Buy Co Inc" or CIK 0000764478
- Access quarterly 10-Q and annual 10-K filings
- Most reliable source for audited financial data
- Historical data available going back many years

### 2. Best Buy Investor Relations
**URL**: https://investors.bestbuy.com/
- Quarterly earnings press releases
- SEC filings section
- Investor presentations with additional insights
- Management commentary on financial results

### 3. Financial Data Platforms
- Yahoo Finance: https://finance.yahoo.com/quote/BBY/financials?p=BBY
- Google Finance: https://www.google.com/finance/
- MarketWatch: https://www.marketwatch.com/investing/stock/bby
- Reuters: https://www.reuters.com/quote/BBY-N/

### 4. Professional Financial Databases
- Bloomberg Terminal (if available)
- Capital IQ
- FactSet
- S&P Capital IQ
- Refinitiv (formerly Thomson Reuters)

## Key Financial Documents to Download

### Quarterly Reports (10-Q)
- Filed approximately 40 days after quarter end
- Contains unaudited financial statements
- Management discussion and analysis
- Quarterly financial highlights

### Annual Reports (10-K)
- Filed approximately 75 days after fiscal year end
- Contains audited financial statements
- Comprehensive risk factors
- Detailed business analysis

### Earnings Releases
- Press releases issued with quarterly results
- Often contain key metrics and guidance
- Management quotes and strategic updates

## Key Metrics to Extract

### Income Statement Items
- Total Revenue/Net Sales
- Cost of Goods Sold
- Gross Profit
- Operating Expenses (SG&A, R&D)
- Operating Income/EBIT
- Net Income
- Earnings Per Share (Basic & Diluted)

### Balance Sheet Items
- Total Assets
- Current Assets (Cash, Inventory, Receivables)
- Total Liabilities
- Current Liabilities
- Shareholders' Equity

### Cash Flow Items
- Operating Cash Flow
- Investing Cash Flow
- Financing Cash Flow
- Free Cash Flow (Operating CF - CapEx)

### Ratios and Derived Metrics
- Gross Margin % (Gross Profit / Revenue)
- Operating Margin % (Operating Income / Revenue)
- Net Margin % (Net Income / Revenue)
- Return on Assets (Net Income / Total Assets)
- Return on Equity (Net Income / Shareholders' Equity)
- Debt-to-Equity Ratio
- Current Ratio
- Inventory Turnover
- Accounts Receivable Days

## Data Extraction Process

1. Download the 10-Q forms for each quarter
2. Extract key financial figures from the condensed consolidated statements
3. Calculate relevant ratios and margins
4. Normalize for any one-time items or accounting changes
5. Organize data in spreadsheet for trend analysis
6. Create visualizations showing trends over time

## Analysis Considerations

### Seasonal Adjustments
- Q4 typically much stronger due to holiday season
- Consider year-over-year comparisons rather than sequential
- Adjust for timing of Black Friday and holiday promotions

### Special Items
- Look for restructuring charges
- Note any asset impairments
- Identify one-time gains or losses
- Adjust for discontinued operations

### Forward Guidance
- Review management's outlook in MD&A sections
- Note any changes in business strategy
- Identify upcoming initiatives that may affect financials

## Validation Steps

1. Cross-reference data across multiple sources
2. Verify mathematical calculations
3. Check for consistency with previous filings
4. Validate against analyst estimates and consensus figures
5. Review footnotes for important accounting policies
